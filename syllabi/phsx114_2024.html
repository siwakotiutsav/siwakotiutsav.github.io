Course Description

This course introduces mechanics, fluids, heat, thermodynamics, and sound waves through hands-on labs and data analysis. Emphasis on quantitative reasoning, uncertainty analysis, and scientific method.

Note: 3.5 years of college-prep math (including trigonometry) recommended.

KU Core 34: Natural & Physical Sciences

Upon completion, students will be able to:

  • Apply the scientific method to experimental data.
  • Use mathematical reasoning for scientific conclusions.
  • Evaluate scientific models and their limitations.
Learning Outcomes
  • Collect data and assess uncertainties.
  • Compare linear vs. nonlinear models.
  • Apply error propagation.
  • Redesign experiments based on results.
Course Topics (Selected Modules)
  • Measurement & Error Analysis
  • Excel Tools for Data Analysis
  • Graphs, Correlations & Curve Fitting
  • Statistical Methods (t-tests, histograms)
  • Lab Modules: R1, M3, F1, Final Assessments
