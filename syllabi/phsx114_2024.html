Course Description

This course introduces the principles and applications of mechanics, fluids, heat, thermodynamics, and sound waves through hands-on experiments and data analysis. Emphasis is placed on developing quantitative reasoning and problem-solving skills through the practice of scientific methods. Students will conduct experiments, analyze data with attention to measurement uncertainty, and use those results to draw conclusions about physical models.

Note: Students should have completed 3.5 years of college-prep math, including trigonometry.

KU Core 34: Natural & Physical Sciences

This course satisfies the KU Core Goal for Natural & Physical Sciences. Upon completion, students will be able to:

  • Apply the scientific method to gather, analyze, and interpret experimental data.
  • Use mathematical reasoning to support conclusions drawn from scientific observations.
  • Understand and evaluate scientific models, assumptions, and limitations.

Learning Outcomes

  • Collect experimental data and determine statistical and systematic uncertainties.
  • Differentiate between linear and nonlinear physical models using correlations and predictions.
  • Apply error propagation techniques to make and evaluate predictions.
  • Iteratively redesign experiments based on findings and uncertainty analysis.

Course Topics (Selected Modules)

  • Measurement and Error Analysis: Uncertainty types, standard deviation, and error propagation.
  • Excel Tools for Data Analysis: Organizing data, formulas, and graphing.
  • Graphs and Correlations: Scatter plots, error bars, residuals, curve fitting.
  • Comparative Statistical Methods: Modified t-tests (Welch's T and T*), histograms.
