Level: B. Pharm./B. Tech. I
Credit Hours: 3
Semester: I

Course Objectives

This course helps first-year undergraduate students develop physical intuition through understanding fundamental concepts in mechanics, optics, heat & thermodynamics, and modern physics, with emphasis on conceptual understanding over complex mathematics.

Grading Policy

Attendance 5%
Homework Problems 10%
Internal Exams (2) 5% + 5%
Practical Exam 25%
Final Exam 50%
Qualification Requirements:
  • Minimum 10% combined from Attendance + Homework + Internal required for final exam eligibility
  • Failure to meet minimum results in NQ (Not Qualified) grade
  • NQ students cannot attempt final examinations
  • Practical exams mandatory regardless of NQ status

Course Structure (Total 45 Hours)

Mechanics 16 hours

Dynamics of System of Particles
  • Work done by constant/variable forces
  • Work-energy theorem
  • Conservative/non-conservative forces
  • Conservation of linear momentum
  • Center of mass & variable mass systems
  • Collisions & angular momentum
  • Rotational kinematics & moment of inertia
Wave and Oscillation
  • Simple harmonic oscillator
  • Damped/forced vibrations
  • Resonance systems

Textbooks

  • Resnick & Halliday, Physics I & II
  • Serway & Jewett, Physics for Engineers
  • Murugeshan, Modern Physics
