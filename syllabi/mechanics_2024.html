Course Objectives

Develop physical intuition through fundamental concepts in mechanics, optics, thermodynamics, and modern physics, emphasizing conceptual understanding over complex mathematics.

Course Structure (45 Hours)

Mechanics 16 hours

Dynamics of System of Particles

  • Work-energy theorem
  • Conservation laws (momentum, angular momentum)
  • Rotational kinematics
  • Moment of inertia calculations

Wave and Oscillation

  • Harmonic oscillators
  • Damped/forced vibrations
  • Resonance phenomena

Optics 10 hours

Interference

  • Young's double-slit
  • Thin film interference
  • Newton's rings

Diffraction

  • Fraunhofer diffraction
  • Diffraction gratings

Thermodynamics 7 hours

Heat Transfer

  • Thermal conductivity
  • Black body radiation

Thermodynamic Laws

  • First & Second laws
  • Heat engines
  • Entropy

Modern Physics 12 hours

Atomic & Molecular Physics

  • Atomic spectra
  • Semiconductor theory

Nuclear Physics

  • Nuclear reactions
  • Radiation applications
  • MRI fundamentals
