Lunar Eclipse Over Lawrence, Kansas

Published: March 15, 2025

Yesterday was a truly magical evening here in Lawrence, Kansas, as the Moon slipped into Earth’s shadow and put on a captivating celestial show. I set up my telescope in my backyard, calibrated the tracking, and captured dozens of frames as the eclipse progressed. Later, I composited the images to showcase each phase-from the subtle penumbral shading to the deep red hue at totality. It was a night I’ll never forget.

Capturing the Hubble Space Telescope Over Kathmandu

Published: January 1, 2024

As I was browsing Facebook during the lockdown, I came across a post by the Nepal Astronomical Society (NASO) predicting a visible pass of the Hubble Space Telescope over Kathmandu. I grabbed my wide-angle lens, hiked up to the Swayambhunath Temple, and waited in the pre-dawn chill. When Hubble’s reflected sunlight streaked across the sky, I fired off a burst of 30-second exposures. The result is a bright arc framing the ancient stupa-an intersection of history and cutting-edge science.

Venus and the Pleiades Celestial Dance

Published: April 2, 2021

On the night of April 3, 2020, Venus slipped into the Pleiades star cluster in a rare conjunction visible to the naked eye. I positioned my 200 mm telephoto lens on a tracking mount and balanced the bright planet against the faint blue glow of the Seven Sisters. The resulting frame captures Venus nestled within the cluster-an elegant dance set against the inky winter sky.

Orion Unbound: Capturing the Hunter in Bhaktapur

Published: October 28, 2020

Orion, the Celestial Hunter, has long been a winter sky icon. From the courtyards of Bhaktapur Durbar Square, I set my tracking mount to follow the constellation’s rise. Over a five-minute exposure, the star fields and nebulae of Orion’s Belt painted an ethereal band above the ancient brick temples. The juxtaposition of Himalayan architecture and glowing cosmos made for an unforgettable scene.
